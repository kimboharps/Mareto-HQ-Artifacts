Cases

Common questions about opening, managing, and closing cases in Mareto. Cases are program-specific containers that track a person's relationship with a service.

Case Management Case Status Enrollment Discharge
Q
How do I open a new case for someone already in the system?

Navigate to their Person record and create a new Case. Select the program, assign staff, and set the case type. The person's demographics carry over automatically — no need to re-enter them.

Q
Can a person have more than one case in the same program?

Yes. For example, a youth could have two separate referrals to the same program at different times. Each case is independent with its own timeline, status, and notes.

Q
What do the case statuses mean (open, pending, on-hold, transferred, closed)?

These may be configured differently for your organization, but generally:

Open — actively receiving services.
Pending — waiting on intake or assessment.
On-Hold — temporarily paused.
Transferred — moved to another program or worker.
Closed — services have concluded.

Note: Check with your administrator for your organization's specific status definitions, as they may differ from the defaults.
Q
What happens when I close a case?

Closing a case in one program does not affect the person's other active cases. Depending on your organization's configuration, discharge automations may cancel future appointments and update consent status.

The closed case and all its history remain accessible for reporting.

Q
Can a closed case be reopened?

Mareto supports soft archiving, which allows temporary file closure with reactivation capability. Whether a specific case can be reopened depends on your organization's configuration and retention policies. Contact your administrator if you need to reactivate a closed case.
