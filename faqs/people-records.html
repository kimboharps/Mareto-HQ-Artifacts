People & Records

Common questions about searching, creating, and managing person records in Mareto's Person Registry. If your question isn't answered here, submit a ticket through the Help Centre.

Q
How do I search for an existing person in the system?

Use the search bar at the top of the Person Registry. You can search by name, and the system uses fuzzy matching — so searching “Bob” will also surface “Robert.”

Tip: Always search before creating a new record. This prevents duplicate entries from being created across programs.
Q
What should I do if I find a duplicate person record?

Do not delete either record. Flag the duplicate with your administrator. Mareto has a file-merging tool that consolidates all cases, events, and history from the duplicate into the correct record — ensuring no data is lost.

Q
How do I link family members together?

From a person's profile, use the Family Linking feature to connect related individuals. Once linked, you can view the entire family unit's engagement across all programs — for example, a parent in one program and a child in another — from a single view.

Q
Can I add a photo to a person's profile?

Yes. Person records support photo uploads. You can also set person type designations (child, youth, adult) on the same profile.

Q
How do I record a contact with someone who isn't a client yet?

Use Pre-Client Contact Tracking to document phone calls, emails, or inquiries. These records are stored separately and will automatically roll into the person's full profile if they are formally onboarded as a client later.

Q
What is the difference between a Person and a Case?

A Person is the individual — one record per person across the entire organization. It holds demographics, contact information, and family connections.

A Case is a container for that person's relationship with a specific program (e.g., a housing case, a counselling file). One person can have multiple cases across multiple programs, or even multiple cases within the same program.

Key concept: Because the Person record is primary, every case and event across every program links back to one individual. This is what makes cross-program visibility possible.
Q
If a person is served by multiple programs, do I need to enter their demographics each time?

No. Demographics are entered once on the Person record and are shared across all programs. When you open a new case for that person, their existing profile information carries over automatically.

Q
I can't see a client's record — why?

Mareto uses role-based access controls that determine what each staff member can see. If a client is in a program outside your assigned access, their record or certain fields may not be visible to you. This is by design to protect client privacy.

Contact your administrator if you believe you should have access to a particular record.

Q
How does the system prevent duplicate records from being created?

Mareto uses fuzzy matching to flag potential duplicates before they are created. If “Robert Smith” already exists and someone tries to enter “Bob Smith,” the system will flag the match and prompt you to review the existing record before proceeding.

External referral URL forms also check for exact name matches before processing submissions.

Q
I need a field or drop-down option that doesn't exist — who do I ask?

Contact your administrator. Mareto is fully configurable, so new fields, drop-down values, and person type designations can be added as your organization's needs evolve. You can also submit a support ticket through the Help Centre.
